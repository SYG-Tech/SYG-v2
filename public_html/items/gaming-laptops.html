What We Look For When Buying Gaming Laptops

Graphics Card (GPU)

The GPU is the most important component in a gaming laptop. Newer NVIDIA RTX or AMD Radeon RX GPUs significantly increase value.

Processor & RAM

Gaming laptops with newer Intel Core i7/i9 or AMD Ryzen 7/9 processors and 16GB+ RAM are valued higher.

Display Quality

High refresh rate displays (144Hz+), higher resolutions, and better panel types (IPS, OLED) increase value.

Storage Type & Size

Gaming laptops with NVMe SSDs and larger storage capacities (512GB+) are more valuable.

Thermal Performance

Gaming laptops with good cooling systems and no overheating issues are valued higher.

Physical Condition

Gaming laptops without cracks, dents, or significant wear will receive higher offers.

How to Prepare Your Gaming Laptop for Sale

  1. Back up your game library and saves: Make sure to back up your game library, save files, and other important data.
  2. Sign out of gaming platforms: Log out of Steam, Epic Games, Battle.net, and other gaming platforms.
  3. Factory reset your laptop: Perform a complete factory reset to remove all personal data and games.
  4. Clean your laptop: Dust out the cooling vents and fans, and clean the exterior and screen.
  5. Gather accessories: Include the original power adapter, any gaming peripherals, and original packaging if available.
  6. Remove password protection: Make sure the laptop is not password protected after reset.
