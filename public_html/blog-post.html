How Much is Your Old iPhone Really Worth? A Comprehensive Guide

By Tech Expert
April 1, 2025
Value Guides

When it's time to upgrade to the latest iPhone, many people wonder how much their current device is worth. The answer isn't always straightforward, as numerous factors affect the resale value of iPhones. In this comprehensive guide, we'll break down exactly what determines your iPhone's value and how you can maximize your return when selling.

Key Factors That Determine iPhone Value

The resale value of an iPhone is influenced by several important factors:

1. Model and Generation

Newer iPhone models naturally command higher prices than older generations. For example, an iPhone 14 Pro will be worth significantly more than an iPhone 11, even if both are in perfect condition. Apple's premium "Pro" models typically retain value better than standard models.

Here's a general breakdown of how different generations compare in value retention:

  • Current generation (iPhone 15 series): 65-75% of original retail price after one year
  • Previous generation (iPhone 14 series): 50-60% of original retail price
  • Two generations old (iPhone 13 series): 40-50% of original retail price
  • Three generations old (iPhone 12 series): 30-40% of original retail price
  • Four or more generations old: Typically less than 30% of original retail price

2. Storage Capacity

iPhones with larger storage capacities (256GB, 512GB, 1TB) generally command higher resale values than their lower-capacity counterparts. This price difference is usually proportional to the original price difference between storage tiers.

Pro Tip: Storage Value

While higher storage models are worth more, the premium you'll get for a 512GB vs. 128GB model is typically less than the original price difference when the phone was new. If you're buying a new iPhone with resale value in mind, the base or middle storage tier often provides the best value retention ratio.

3. Condition

The physical condition of your iPhone is perhaps the most significant factor affecting its resale value. Devices are typically categorized into condition tiers:

  • Mint/Like New: No scratches, dents, or signs of use. Original packaging and accessories included. These can fetch 90-95% of the value of a comparable new device.
  • Excellent: Minor signs of use but no significant scratches or damage. These typically command 80-90% of top value.
  • Good: Noticeable wear and minor scratches, but fully functional. These fetch around 70-80% of top value.
  • Fair: Obvious signs of use, significant scratches or minor dents, but still fully functional. These typically bring 50-70% of top value.
  • Poor: Major cosmetic damage, cracks, or functionality issues. These may only fetch 30-50% of top value.

Important Note: Screen Damage

A cracked screen can reduce your iPhone's value by 50% or more. If your screen has minor cracks, it might be worth repairing before selling, but do the math firstâ€”sometimes the repair cost exceeds the value increase.

4. Functionality

All features of your iPhone must work properly to get top dollar. Issues with the following components will significantly reduce value:

  • Touchscreen responsiveness
  • Battery health (below 80% capacity is considered degraded)
  • Cameras (front and rear)
  • Speakers and microphones
  • Buttons (volume, power, home button/Touch ID)
  • Face ID or Touch ID functionality
  • Charging port
  • Wireless capabilities (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular)

5. Carrier Status

Unlocked iPhones that can work with any carrier command the highest prices. Carrier-locked devices are worth less because they limit the potential buyer pool. Additionally:

  • Factory unlocked models (purchased directly from Apple) typically have the highest value
  • Carrier unlocked models (originally purchased from a carrier but later unlocked) have slightly lower value
  • Carrier locked models have the lowest value

6. Included Accessories

Having the original box, charger, cables, and unused EarPods can increase your iPhone's resale value by 5-10%. While not a major factor, it does make a difference, especially for newer models.

Value Maximizing Tip

The best time to sell your iPhone is typically 1-2 months before a new iPhone release (usually in September). Prices for current models drop significantly once new models are announced.

How to Get the Most Money for Your iPhone

Follow these tips to maximize your iPhone's resale value:

1. Timing is Everything

As mentioned above, sell your iPhone before the next generation is announced. iPhone values typically drop 15-20% within the first month after a new model release.

2. Keep It Protected

Use a case and screen protector from day one. iPhones in excellent condition can fetch up to 25% more than those with visible wear.

3. Maintain Battery Health

Use optimized battery charging settings and avoid frequent fast charging to maintain battery health. iPhones with battery health above 85% are worth significantly more.

4. Factory Reset Properly

Before selling, properly back up your data and perform a factory reset. Make sure to turn off Find My iPhone and sign out of iCloud to avoid activation lock issues.

5. Compare Buyback Options

Shop around for the best offer. Check multiple buyback services, carrier trade-in programs, and private selling options. At SellYourGadgets.com, we offer a price match guarantee plus an additional 5% over any legitimate competitor's offer.

6. Consider Repair vs. Selling As-Is

For major issues like screen damage, calculate whether the cost of repair will be offset by the increase in buyback value. Sometimes it's better to sell as-is, while other times a repair can net you more money overall.

Conclusion

Your iPhone's resale value depends on a combination of model, condition, functionality, and market timing. By understanding these factors and taking good care of your device, you can maximize its value when it's time to upgrade.
