What We Look For When Buying Video Games

Game Condition

Games with minimal scratches on discs, intact cartridges, and complete packaging will receive higher offers.

Original Packaging

Games with original cases, manuals, and inserts are valued higher than disc-only or cartridge-only games.

Rarity & Demand

Limited editions, rare titles, and games with high demand will fetch premium prices.

Release Date

Newer releases typically have higher value, but classic and retro games can also be valuable.

Functionality

Games that load and play properly without errors will receive higher offers.

Special Features

Games with bonus content, DLC codes (unused), or special editions can be worth more.

How to Prepare Your Video Games for Sale

  1. Clean your games: Gently clean discs with a soft, lint-free cloth. For cartridges, use compressed air to remove dust.
  2. Organize packaging: Gather original cases, manuals, and inserts if you have them.
  3. Test functionality: Make sure games load and play properly before bringing them in.
  4. Remove personal data: For games with save data, consider whether you want to keep your progress by backing it up.
  5. Bundle accessories: Include any related accessories or peripherals specific to the game.
  6. Organize by platform: Sort your games by console to make the evaluation process faster.
