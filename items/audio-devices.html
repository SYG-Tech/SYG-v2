What We Look For When Buying Audio Devices

Model & Age

Newer models from popular brands like Apple, Sony, and Bose typically fetch higher prices than older or less popular models.

Physical Condition

Audio devices without scratches, cracks, or other damage will receive higher offers.

Battery Health

Wireless devices with good battery health that hold a charge well are valued higher.

Sound Quality

Devices that produce clear sound without distortion or other issues will receive higher offers.

Connectivity

Wireless devices that connect properly via Bluetooth or Wi-Fi are valued higher.

Accessories

Including original charging cases, cables, ear tips, and packaging can increase the value of your audio devices.

How to Prepare Your Audio Devices for Sale

  1. Clean your devices: Gently clean your headphones, earbuds, or speakers to remove dirt, dust, and earwax.
  2. Unpair from devices: Disconnect your audio devices from your phone, tablet, or computer.
  3. Reset to factory settings: If possible, reset your audio devices to clear any personal settings.
  4. Charge fully: Make sure wireless devices are fully charged before bringing them in.
  5. Gather accessories: Include charging cases, cables, ear tips, and original packaging if available.
  6. Test functionality: Make sure all features are working properly before bringing them in.
