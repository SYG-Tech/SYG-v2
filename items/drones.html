What We Look For When Buying Drones

Model & Age

Newer models from popular brands like DJI and Autel typically fetch higher prices than older or less popular models.

Camera Quality

Drones with higher resolution cameras (4K/8K) and better gimbals are valued higher.

Physical Condition

Drones without crashes, damage, or repairs will receive higher offers.

Battery Health

Drones with good battery health and multiple batteries are valued higher.

Functionality

Drones that fly properly with all features working will receive higher offers.

Accessories

Including controllers, extra batteries, propellers, cases, and original packaging can increase the value of your drone.

How to Prepare Your Drone for Sale

  1. Back up your footage: Make sure all your photos and videos are transferred to your computer or cloud storage.
  2. Format memory cards: After backing up, format your memory cards to remove all images and videos.
  3. Reset drone settings: Restore your drone to factory settings to clear custom configurations.
  4. Unlink from DJI account: Remove the drone from your DJI account or other manufacturer accounts.
  5. Clean your drone: Gently clean the body, propellers, and camera lens.
  6. Gather accessories: Include batteries, chargers, controllers, propellers, and original packaging if available.
  7. Check functionality: Make sure all features are working properly before bringing it in.
