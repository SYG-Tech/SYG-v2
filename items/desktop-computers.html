What We Look For When Buying Desktop Computers

Processor (CPU)

Desktops with newer Intel Core i5/i7/i9 or AMD Ryzen 5/7/9 processors are valued higher than those with older or entry-level CPUs.

Graphics Card (GPU)

Desktops with dedicated graphics cards, especially newer NVIDIA RTX or AMD Radeon RX models, are worth significantly more.

RAM & Storage

More RAM (16GB+) and larger SSDs (512GB+) increase the value of your desktop computer.

Form Factor & Brand

All-in-ones, premium brands, and specialty builds (like gaming PCs) typically fetch higher prices than standard towers.

Physical Condition

Desktops without damage, with clean components, and in good working order will receive higher offers.

Accessories

Including original peripherals, cables, and packaging can increase the value of your desktop computer.

How to Prepare Your Desktop Computer for Sale

  1. Back up your data: Make sure all your important files, photos, and documents are backed up to an external drive or cloud service.
  2. Sign out of accounts: Log out of all personal accounts and services.
  3. Factory reset or wipe your drive: Perform a complete factory reset or secure drive wipe to remove all personal data.
  4. Clean your desktop: Dust out the interior components and clean the exterior to make it look its best.
  5. Gather accessories: Include power cables, peripherals, and original packaging if available.
  6. Remove password protection: Make sure the computer is not password protected after reset.
